The Beauty of the Internet and Products

December 2, 2020 · 2 min · Howsun
    Built with ConvertKit

    Lately, I traveled to Taiwan. Packing has been a big problem for me whenever I travel, especially if I have to stay at the destination location for a longer period of time.

    I am an avid book reader and rely on books for work a lot. It’s troubling because physical books are heavy and could easily make luggage go overweight; it doesn’t help when I want to bring a small library (I try to limit myself to 15 books now) with me.

    So I often wonder if I am the only person that has this problem. All the books on Kindle are not enough? Who needs that many books in just two months anyway?

    And this happens again that during this recent trip, I need to get to two of books about web design, but they were nowhere to be found. They were not among the chosen ones when I packed (?), but I remembered I put them in the luggage. Did I make the wrong choice on books…? I really thought I brought them along with me…

    Since I need get the information from those books, I googled: “[book name], summary”. Lo and behold, Blinkist’s ad showed up on the top of the search results. I clicked the link, seeing the key ideas are decently laid out, thinking I might use it. Magical – that’s just the first impression, because I haven’t used Blinkist, so can’t say how good it is yet.

    But that’s the beautiful thing about the internet and products. The internet make distribution simple. Products are built to solve problems, albeit how trivial the problem may seem and how random the request stem from.
